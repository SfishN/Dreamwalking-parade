This project explores the fragile boundary between constructed reality and internal perception through a generative audiovisual system. Inspired by dream logic and cinematic references such as *Paprika*, the work creates a dynamic environment where fragments continuously emerge, transform, and collapse. The system operates through coded rules that simulate a shifting mental landscape, allowing the user to interact with and destabilize the structure.

At its core, the project reflects on how memory, identity, and perception are not fixed, but constantly reconstructed through interaction and time. Visual elements behave like unstable architectures—rotating, dissolving, and reassembling—while sound operates as both a trigger and a consequence of these transformations. As the system reaches a threshold, it enters a collapse state, where previously coherent forms break down into chaotic sequences, resembling a “life flashing before your eyes” moment.

Critically, this work questions the illusion of control within digital systems and interactive media. While the user appears to influence the environment, the underlying logic reveals a pre-defined structure that inevitably leads toward collapse. This tension between agency and determinism reflects broader concerns in contemporary digital culture, where participation is often framed as freedom, yet constrained by invisible systems. The project invites users to confront this paradox through an immersive, unstable experience.
